                    1. SYSTEM OVERVIEW & CHARACTER SHEET BASICS
================================================================================

WHAT IS THIS SYSTEM?
-------------------
The RPG Skill Tree System is a character builder and management tool that creates
detailed character sheets for TTRPG campaigns. Each character sheet contains:

- Complete stat blocks with combat modifiers
- Skill trees with prerequisites and costs
- Equipment and inventory management
- Racial abilities and special features
- 166+ pre-made characters ready to use as enemies or NPCs

HOW TO USE CHARACTER SHEETS IN TTRPG:
------------------------------------
1. **Player Characters**: Players create characters using the web application
2. **Export Characters**: Save character data as JSON files
3. **Import in Campaign**: Load character sheets for reference during games
4. **Enemy Characters**: Use pre-made characters as combat encounters
5. **NPCs**: Use pre-made characters as non-player characters

CHARACTER SHEET STRUCTURE:
-------------------------
Each character contains:
- **Basic Info**: Name, race, level, experience
- **Stats**: HP, Stamina, Strength, Magic Power, Accuracy, Speed, Defenses
- **Skills**: Unlocked abilities organized by category
- **Equipment**: Weapons, armor, accessories with stat bonuses
- **Inventory**: Items, consumables, materials
- **Notes**: Character backstory, campaign notes, etc.

                        2. COMBAT MECHANICS & DICE ROLLING
================================================================================

CORE COMBAT SYSTEM:
------------------
This system uses a d20-based combat system with the following mechanics:

**ATTACK ROLLS:**
- Roll: d20 + Accuracy + Skill Bonuses
- Target: Must EQUAL or EXCEED enemy's Physical/Magical Defence
- Critical Hit: Natural 20 = automatic hit + double damage

**DAMAGE CALCULATION:**
- Physical Damage: Weapon Damage + Strength Modifier
- Magical Damage: Spell Damage + Magic Power Modifier
- Defense: Physical/Magical Defence reduces incoming damage

**INITIATIVE:**
- Order: Highest Speed goes first, If 2 or more are the same, GM decides
- Surprise: Stealth characters get advantage on first round

DICE ROLLING EXAMPLES:
--------------------
**Example 1 - Sword Attack:**
- Player rolls: d20 + 5 (Accuracy) + 2 (Sword Skill) = 18
- Enemy Defence: 15
- Result: HIT! (18 > 15)
- Damage: d8 (Sword) + 3 (Strength) = 8 damage

**Example 2 - Fireball Spell:**
- Player rolls: d20 + 4 (Accuracy) + 3 (Fire Magic) = 16
- Enemy Magical Defence: 14
- Result: HIT! (16 > 14)
- Damage: 2d6 (Fireball) + 4 (Magic Power) = 12 damage

**Example 3 - Critical Hit:**
- Player rolls: Natural 20
- Result: Automatic hit + Additional Dice Roll
- Damage: (d8 + 3) + d8 = 19 maximum damage

                        3. CHARACTER STATS & HOW TO USE THEM
================================================================================

PRIMARY STATISTICS:
------------------

**HEALTH (HP):**
- Function: How much damage character can take
- Usage: Track during combat, reaches 0 = unconscious
- Upgrades: Cost 3 Lumens each
- Equipment: Armor can increase maximum HP

**STAMINA:**
- Function: Cost to use skills in combat
- Usage: Some skills cost 2-3 stamina, toggle skills cost per turn
- Upgrades: Cost 5 Lumens each
- Recovery: Rest or use stamina potions

**STRENGTH:**
- Function: Added to Physical Damage rolls
- Usage: When rolling Physical Damage, add Strength score
- Range: -3 to +8 (upgradeable with Lumens)
- Equipment: Weapons and gear can boost beyond limits

**MAGIC POWER:**
- Function: Added to Magical Damage rolls
- Usage: When rolling Magical Damage, add Magic Power score
- Range: -3 to +8 (upgradeable with Lumens)
- Equipment: Staves and magical items can boost beyond limits

**ACCURACY:**
- Function: Added to hit attempts
- Usage: When rolling to hit enemy, add Accuracy score
- Range: -3 to +8 (upgradeable with Lumens)
- Critical: Higher accuracy = better chance to hit

**SPEED:**
- Function: Agility and reflexes
- Usage: Initiative, dodging, movement
- Range: 2 to +8 (upgradeable with Lumens)
- Equipment: Light armor and accessories can boost

**PHYSICAL DEFENCE:**
- Function: Target number for physical attacks
- Usage: Enemy's attack roll must EQUAL or EXCEED this number
- Range: 8 to +8 (upgradeable with Lumens)
- Equipment: Armor and shields provide major bonuses

**MAGICAL DEFENCE:**
- Function: Target number for magical attacks
- Usage: Enemy's spell roll must EQUAL or EXCEED this number
- Range: 8 to +8 (upgradeable with Lumens)
- Equipment: Magical items and certain armor types help

STAT MODIFIERS IN GAMEPLAY:
--------------------------
- **Positive Stats**: Add to relevant rolls
- **Negative Stats**: Subtract from relevant rolls
- **Zero Stats**: No modifier (neutral)
- **Equipment Bonuses**: Can exceed Lumen-purchased limits

================================================================================
                        5. LOOT & REWARD SYSTEM
================================================================================

DYNAMIC LOOT GENERATION:
-----------------------
When enemies are defeated, they drop loot based on their level and skills:

**Loot Tiers by Monster Level:**
- **Level 1-3**: 3 Common, 1 Uncommon, 1 Rare items
- **Level 4-7**: 2 Common, 2 Uncommon, 1 Rare items
- **Level 8-10**: 3 Uncommon, 2 Rare, 1 Epic items
- **Level 11+**: 4 Uncommon, 3 Rare, 2 Epic items

**Skill-Based Loot:**
- Fire monsters drop fire-themed items
- Sword-wielding enemies drop weapons
- Magic users drop magical items and essences
- Defensive creatures drop armor and shields

**Lumen Rewards:**
- Base: Monster Level × 5 Lumens
- Party Scaling: 1 player (×1.0), 2-3 players (×1.5), 4-5 players (×2.0), 6+ players (×2.5)
- Example: Level 5 monster, 4 players = 5 × 5 × 2.0 = 50 Lumens

**Money Rewards:**
- Quests and story rewards provide Money (Gil)
- Used for equipment purchases in shops
- Separate from Lumen (which is for skill progression)

================================================================================
                        6. SKILL SYSTEM & CHARACTER PROGRESSION
================================================================================

SKILL CATEGORIES:
----------------

**WEAPON SKILLS:**
- Sword, Axe, Staff, Dagger, Polearm, Hammer, Unarmed, Bow
- Each weapon type has 5 tiers of skills
- Prerequisites: Must unlock lower tiers before higher ones
- Costs: 3L → 8L → 15L → 25L → 35L → 50L

**MAGIC SKILLS:**
- Fire, Ice, Thunder, Earth, Wind, Water, Darkness, Light
- Elemental spells and abilities
- Some skills have elemental resistances
- Toggle skills require stamina maintenance

**PROFESSION SKILLS:**
- Smithing, Alchemy, Enchanting, Cooking, Archaeology, Herbalism
- Crafting and utility abilities
- Can create items and consumables
- Useful for downtime activities

**RACIAL SKILLS:**
- Unique to each race
- Humans can learn Tier 1 skills from other races
- Provide passive bonuses and special abilities
- Examples: Elven Accuracy, Dwarven Toughness, Orcish Fury

**MONSTER SKILLS:**
- Defense, Combat, Magic, Utility categories
- Special abilities for monster characters
- Can be used when playing monster characters

**TOGGLE SKILLS:**
- Special abilities that require stamina to maintain
- Activation Cost: Pay stamina upfront (2-3 stamina)
- Maintenance Cost: Pay stamina each turn (1-2 stamina)
- Auto-deactivate if insufficient stamina
- Examples: Defensive Stance, Berserker Rage, Fire Staff

SKILL PROGRESSION:
-----------------
**Lumen Economy:**
- Starting Lumens: 150
- Earned by: Defeating enemies, completing quests
- Spent on: Unlocking skills, upgrading stats

**Prerequisites:**
- Must unlock lower tier skills before higher ones
- Some skills require skills from other categories
- Racial restrictions apply (except for humans)

**Skill Bonuses:**
- Each skill provides stat bonuses
- Bonuses stack with equipment
- Some skills provide special abilities

                        8. EQUIPMENT & INVENTORY MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

EQUIPMENT SYSTEM:
----------------

**Weapon Types:**
- Swords: Balanced damage, good for most situations
- Axes: High damage, slower attacks
- Staves: Magical focus, good for spellcasters
- Daggers: Fast attacks, good for rogues
- Polearms: Reach weapons, good for defense
- Hammers: High damage, good against armor
- Unarmed: No weapon needed, good for monks
- Bows: Ranged attacks, good for archers

**Armor Types:**
- Light Armor: Low protection, no speed penalty
- Medium Armor: Moderate protection, slight speed penalty
- Heavy Armor: High protection, significant speed penalty
- Magical Armor: Special properties and resistances

**Accessories:**
- Rings: Magical bonuses and special abilities
- Amulets: Protective properties and resistances
- Belts: Utility items and stat bonuses
- Boots: Movement bonuses and special abilities

**Equipment Bonuses:**
- Can boost stats beyond Lumen-purchased limits
- Stack with skill bonuses
- Essential for high-level optimization
- Can provide special abilities and resistances

INVENTORY MANAGEMENT:
--------------------

**Item Categories:**
- Weapons: Combat equipment
- Armor: Protective equipment
- Accessories: Special equipment
- Consumables: Potions, scrolls, food
- Materials: Crafting components
- Quest Items: Story-related items

**Carrying Capacity:**
- Based on Strength stat
- Heavy items may reduce movement
- Some items have special storage requirements

**Item Rarity:**
- Common: Basic equipment, easy to find
- Uncommon: Better equipment, moderate rarity
- Rare: Powerful equipment, hard to find
- Epic: Legendary equipment, very rare
- Legendary: Mythical equipment, extremely rare

                        10. TROUBLESHOOTING & BALANCE TIPS
================================================================================

COMMON ISSUES:
--------------

**Characters Too Weak:**
- Check if they have appropriate equipment
- Ensure they've spent Lumens on skills and stats
- Consider giving them better starting equipment
- Adjust encounter difficulty downward

**Characters Too Strong:**
- Increase enemy levels and abilities
- Use higher-level monsters
- Add environmental challenges
- Reduce loot rewards

**Combat Too Easy:**
- Increase enemy Physical/Magical Defence
- Add more enemies to encounters
- Use tactical positioning and cover
- Add status effects and special abilities

**Combat Too Hard:**
- Decrease enemy defences
- Reduce enemy damage output
- Provide healing opportunities
- Give players tactical advantages

BALANCE TIPS:
------------

**Encounter Design:**
- Use multiple weaker enemies instead of one strong enemy
- Vary enemy types to challenge different character builds
- Include environmental hazards and tactical elements
- Provide opportunities for creative problem-solving

**Reward Distribution:**
- Balance Lumen rewards with character progression needs
- Provide equipment upgrades regularly
- Include consumables and utility items
- Reward creative thinking and roleplay

**Character Progression:**
- Ensure all players can contribute meaningfully
- Provide opportunities for different skill trees
- Balance combat and non-combat challenges
- Allow for character customization and specialization

**Campaign Pacing:**
- Start with simple encounters and build complexity
- Provide downtime for character development
- Include social and exploration challenges
- Build toward meaningful climaxes

                                CONCLUSION
================================================================================

This system provides a comprehensive framework for running TTRPG campaigns with
detailed character sheets and pre-made enemies. The key to success is:

1. **Understand the mechanics** - Know how stats, skills, and equipment work
2. **Use appropriate encounters** - Match enemy levels to party capabilities
3. **Provide meaningful rewards** - Balance Lumen, Money, and equipment rewards
4. **Encourage creativity** - Let players use skills and equipment creatively
5. **Maintain balance** - Adjust difficulty based on party performance

The 166+ pre-made characters provide endless possibilities for encounters,
NPCs, and campaign scenarios. Use them as inspiration and adapt them to fit
your campaign's needs.

Remember: The goal is to create an engaging, challenging, and fun experience
for all players. Use this system as a foundation, but don't be afraid to
modify and adapt it to suit your specific campaign and players.

Happy gaming!
